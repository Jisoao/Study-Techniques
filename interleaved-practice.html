The Interleaved Practice is the strategy of mixing different topics or types of problems in a single study session. Instead of studying one subject in large chunks (called “blocked” practice), interleaving forces your brain to distinguish between similar concepts and choose the right method—improving problem-solving and long-term understanding.

➤
STRUCTURE

Ideal For: Math, Science, Programming, and any subject with problem types

Tools: Practice worksheets, textbook problems, flashcards

Time Required: 30–60 minutes per session

Complements: Active Recall and Pomodoro

◀ ➤
Steps to Master Interleaved Practice
◀ ➤
STEP 1

Select multiple related topics

Choose 2–4 related but distinct topics (e.g., Algebra, Geometry, Trigonometry). The key is that they should require different methods to solve.

◀ ➤
STEP 2

Mix questions from each topic

Don’t study all of one topic and then move to the next. Instead, alternate problems randomly. For example: solve one algebra question, then one geometry, then one trigonometry.

◀ ➤
STEP 3

Identify the method before solving

Before answering, ask: “What kind of problem is this? What method should I use?” This trains your brain to recognize problem types and strengthens decision-making.

◀ ➤
STEP 4

Review what you got wrong

After solving a set, go over your answers and pinpoint any confusion. Did you misapply a method because topics were too similar? That’s good—it means the technique is working.

◀ ➤
STEP 5

Reflect and adjust

After each session, write down what you learned. Interleaving is challenging at first, but over time, it greatly improves your ability to switch between topics smoothly.

◀ ➤

Congratulations, you have mastered Interleaved Practice!
