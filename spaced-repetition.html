Spaced Repetition is a powerful method that optimizes the timing of reviews to boost long-term memory retention. Instead of cramming the same material repeatedly, you space out reviews over increasing intervals—just as you're about to forget. It's scientifically proven to help you remember more with less time and effort.

➤

STRUCTURE

Ideal For: Vocabulary, definitions, dates, formulas

Tools: Anki, Quizlet, calendar, notebook

Time Required: 5–20 minutes per day

Complements: Active Recall

◀ ➤

Steps to Master Spaced Repetition

◀ ➤

STEP 1

Create review materials

Turn your study material into flashcards or Q&A style notes. Each card should contain only one idea or concept.

◀ ▶

STEP 2

Use a spaced repetition schedule

Apps like Anki will automatically handle the timing for you based on how well you remember each card. For manual learners, try this schedule:

  • 1st review: Day 1
  • 2nd review: Day 3
  • 3rd review: Day 7
  • 4th review: Day 14, and so on.
◀ ▶

STEP 3

Rate your memory strength

After reviewing each card, rate how well you remembered it. Mark the ones you forgot as "hard" or "again," and the ones you remembered easily as "good" or "easy." This controls how often they appear again.

◀ ▶

STEP 4

Stay consistent

Set aside time each day for reviews. Even 10–15 minutes a day can produce amazing results over weeks.

◀ ▶

STEP 5

Update and grow your deck

As you cover new topics, keep adding them into your deck. Spaced Repetition becomes more powerful the longer you use it.

◀ ▶

Congratulations, you have mastered Spaced Repetition!
